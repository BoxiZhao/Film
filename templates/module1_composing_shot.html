Composing your shot

Rule of Thirds

The rule of thirds divides your frame into nine equal parts using two horizontal and two vertical lines. Placing key elements along these lines or at their intersections creates visual interest and balance. This simple but powerful guideline helps you move beyond centering every subject and creates more dynamic compositions.

In this example, the tree is aligned along the right vertical third line. Also, rather than centering the horizon, the horizon is aligned along the topmost third line.

Leading Lines

Leading lines are visual paths that guide the viewer's eye through your image toward your main subject. Look for natural lines in your environmentâ€”roads, fences, buildings, or even patterns of light and shadow. Position these lines to draw attention to your focal point, creating depth and visual flow.

Framing

Use elements in your scene to create a "frame within a frame" around your subject. Doorways, windows, tree branches, or architectural features can add context, depth, and focus to your image. Effective framing draws attention to your subject while adding layers of visual interest and storytelling to your composition.

In this oft-replicated photo, Toronto's CN Tower is framed by two buildings, bringing into focus.
