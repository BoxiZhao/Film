Introduction

Film photography is a worthwhile pursuit that emphasises intentionality. By limiting the shots per roll and removing instant feedback, film forces you to slow down, study the light, and compose carefully.

Differences from digital include:

  • Latent image – exposure is recorded chemically and revealed later in development.
  • Dynamic range – colour‑negative film handles highlights gracefully.
  • Film stock character – each emulsion has unique colour and grain.
