Introduction

 

Film photography endures as a meaningful pursuit in our digital age, offering a deliberate and tactile experience that slows down the creative process. The limited number of exposures on each roll encourages thoughtful composition and patience, while the distinctive grain, color rendition, and imperfections of film create images with a timeless character that digital sensors struggle to replicate. The anticipation of developing and discovering your photographs adds an element of surprise and satisfaction that instant digital feedback cannot match.

This course will teach you the basics of operating a film camera, composing a shot, as well as how to develop film at home. At the end of each module, there will be questions to test your knowledge.
